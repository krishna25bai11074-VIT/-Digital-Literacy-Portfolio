# Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

## What is UPI Fraud?
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of cybercrime where 
attackers trick victims into transferring money or revealing sensitive 
payment credentials through deceptive means. It is one of the fastest 
growing cybercrimes in India due to the widespread adoption of UPI 
platforms like Google Pay, PhonePe, and Paytm.

## Real Incident
In 2022, multiple cases were reported across India where fraudsters 
posed as bank customer care executives. Victims received calls from 
people claiming their UPI account would be blocked unless they 
verified their details immediately. The callers asked victims to 
share their UPI PIN, OTP, or to scan a QR code sent via WhatsApp. 
Several victims lost anywhere between ₹10,000 to ₹5 lakhs within 
minutes of the call. These incidents were widely reported by 
outlets including The Hindu and NDTV.

## How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)
1. Fraudster contacts the victim via phone/WhatsApp posing as a 
   bank official or customer care agent
2. They create a sense of urgency — "Your account will be blocked 
   in 2 hours"
3. Victim is asked to share OTP, UPI PIN, or scan a QR code
4. Once the victim complies, money is instantly transferred out
5. The fraudster disconnects and becomes untraceable

## Who is Usually Targeted?
- First-time UPI users, especially students and senior citizens
- People who have recently posted about financial transactions 
  on social media
- Small business owners using UPI for daily transactions

## Consequences
- Immediate financial loss — often within seconds
- Victims face difficulty recovering money as transactions 
  are instant and irreversible
- Psychological stress, anxiety, and loss of trust in 
  digital payment systems
- Legal complications in tracing anonymous fraudsters
